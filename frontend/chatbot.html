Why Check Your Air Quality Risk?

“Air pollution contributes to over 7 million premature deaths globally every year.”

Even short-term exposure to poor air can affect your breathing, trigger asthma, and increase your risk of heart problems—especially in children, seniors, and those with existing conditions.

How Our Assessment Works

  • Answer a few simple questions about your health and lifestyle.
  • Our tool analyzes your exposure, health status, and local pollution levels.
  • Get a clear risk score and personalized advice for reducing your risk.

Prevention Tips

  • Limit outdoor activity during high AQI days.
  • Use masks or air purifiers indoors.
  • Keep windows closed when pollution is high.
  • Monitor symptoms like cough or breathlessness and seek medical advice if they worsen.

Fast Facts

  • Children and the elderly are most at risk from poor air quality.
  • Smoking and outdoor jobs increase air pollution exposure.
  • Simple changes can reduce your risk by up to 50%.
